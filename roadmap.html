Interview Roadmap

Understand exactly what happens at every stage of a tech interview process — from application to offer letter.

💡

What to Expect

Most tech companies follow a 5-6 round interview process. Each round eliminates candidates, so preparation for each stage is crucial. On average, only 1-3% of applicants receive an offer at top companies.

1
📝 Round 1
Online Application + Resume Screening
The first filter. Your resume is scanned by ATS (Applicant Tracking System) software before a human ever sees it. Keywords, formatting, and relevance matter enormously here.
  • Use keywords directly from the job description
  • Keep resume to 1 page if you have under 3 years experience
  • Use standard section headings (Education, Experience, Skills)
  • Quantify achievements: "Improved load time by 40%" not "Made it faster"
  • No photos, no fancy graphics — ATS can't read them

2
📞 Round 2
Aptitude / Online Assessment Test
Most companies use platforms like HackerRank, Codility, or AMCAT to filter candidates. This round usually includes aptitude, coding, and sometimes verbal/logical reasoning.
  • Practice DSA basics: Arrays, Strings, Sorting, Searching
  • Solve 2-3 LeetCode Easy problems daily for 2 weeks before applying
  • Practice mental math and logical reasoning on IndiaBix or PrepInsta
  • Read questions fully before coding — time management is key
  • Write working code first, optimize later
3
💻 Round 3
Technical Interview — Round 1
A 45-60 minute video/in-person call with a software engineer. Expect live coding, data structures, algorithms, and questions about your projects.
  • Think out loud — interviewers want to see your process, not just the answer
  • Start with a brute-force solution, then optimize
  • Know time & space complexity for all solutions
  • Be ready to explain every line of code on your resume
  • Ask clarifying questions before coding
  • Practice on a whiteboard or paper — not just an IDE
4
🏗️ Round 4
Technical Interview — Round 2 (System Design)
For mid-senior roles. You're asked to design a system like "Design Twitter" or "Build a URL shortener". Tests your understanding of scalability, databases, APIs, and architecture.
  • Clarify requirements and scale (users, requests/second) first
  • Draw a high-level diagram before going deep
  • Know: Load Balancers, Caching, SQL vs NoSQL, CDN, Message Queues
  • Discuss trade-offs for every decision you make
  • For freshers: this may be a deeper CS concepts round instead
5
🧠 Round 5
Managerial / Behavioral Round
A conversation with a team lead or manager. They're assessing your soft skills, cultural fit, teamwork ability, and how you handle challenges.
  • Use the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result
  • Prepare 5-6 stories: leadership, conflict, failure, success, learning
  • Research the company culture and values beforehand
  • Show genuine curiosity — ask smart questions about the team
  • Be honest about weaknesses, but always show what you're doing to improve
6
🤝 Round 6
HR Round — Offer Negotiation
The final round. HR confirms your background, discusses compensation, joining date, and company policies. This is also where you negotiate your salary.
  • Research salary ranges on Glassdoor, LinkedIn Salary before the call
  • Always negotiate — most offers have 10-20% flexibility
  • Be enthusiastic but not desperate
  • Ask about growth opportunities, team culture, and onboarding
  • Get the offer in writing before resigning from your current role
  • Thank the interviewer — it leaves a lasting impression
